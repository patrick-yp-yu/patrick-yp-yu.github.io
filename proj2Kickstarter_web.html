Kickstarter Projects Analysis

Abstract

This is a class project in CSCE489 Data Science and Analytics at Texas A&M University. The project goal is to acquire some investment insights from the top 4000 Kickstarter projects. These crowdfunding projects gained plenty of popularities from backers and successfully turned ideas into mass production. After applying skills of web scraping, data mining, and data trending, we concluded the following observations and suggestions for both creators and investors.

  1. The power of crowdfunding: top 4000 Kickstarter projects received funding that is 4.6 times than their original goal.
  2. The higher number of backers is not equal to the higher number of funding amount.
  3. What kind of Kickstarter projects receive more pledge? The top 3 categories are Product Design, Tabletop Games, and Video Games.
  4. What kind of Kickstarter projects attract people to support? The top 3 categories are Video Games, Product Design, and Tabletop Games.

Introduction¶

For many start-up companies, crowdfunding has become an important way to raise initial capital. Kickstarter is a fast growing platform to turn small projects into a mass production. Project creators can present their novelty, get funding, and create successful products. However, there are tons of projects on Kickstarter. How do project creators distinguish their project to catch peoples attentions? What are the buzzwords in popular projects? How do creators set a reachable funding goal? What are the key elements in a successful project?

This spotlight notebook are going to analyze current Kickstarter projects and Kickstarter projects that successfully got funding. The use data mining skills and data visualization to find valuable insights.

import re

def IsInUS(item):
    states = ['IA', 'KS', 'UT', 'VA', 'NC', 'NE', 'SD', 'AL', 'ID', 'FM', 'DE', 'AK', 'CT', 'PR', 'NM', 'MS', 'PW', 'CO', 'NJ', 'FL', 'MN', 'VI', 'NV', 'AZ', 'WI', 'ND', 'PA', 'OK', 'KY', 'RI', 'NH', 'MO', 'ME', 'VT', 'GA', 'GU', 'AS', 'NY', 'CA', 'HI', 'IL', 'TN', 'MA', 'OH', 'MD', 'MI', 'WY', 'WA', 'OR', 'MH', 'SC', 'IN', 'LA', 'MP', 'DC', 'MT', 'AR', 'WV', 'TX']
    pattern = re.compile(r'\b(' + '|'.join(states) + r')\b', re.IGNORECASE)
    match = pattern.search(item)
    if match:
        flag = 'US'

    return flag
